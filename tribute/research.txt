1911, Mondrian moved to Paris and changed his name, dropping an "a" from "Mondriaan", to emphasize his departure from the Netherlands, the influence of the Cubist style of Pablo Picasso and Georges Braque appeared almost immediately in Mondrian's work.
1914, While visiting the Netherlands World War I began, forcing him to remain there for the duration of the conflict.
1918, Mondrian returned to France, where he fully embraced the art of pure abstraction for the rest of his life.
1919, Mondiran makes his first grid-based painting.
1921, Mondrian's paintings arrive to their definitive and mature form. Thick black lines now separate the forms, which are larger and fewer in number, and more of the forms are left white.
